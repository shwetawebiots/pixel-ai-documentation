Email Campaigns

The Campaigns module allows you to create and manage professional email sequences. Before you can launch your first campaign, ensure your email provider is correctly configured.

Prerequisite: Email Configuration

Properly configuring your email provider is essential for ensuring high deliverability. You can choose between traditional SMTP or use the SendGrid API for better performance.

1. Provider Settings
  • Provider Engine: Select "SMTP Direct (Nodemailer)" for standard mail servers or "SendGrid API" for professional delivery services.
  • Sender Identity: Define the From Name (e.g., "PixelAI Support") and the From Email (e.g., "support@yourdomain.com") that subscribers will see.
  • SMTP Settings: Enter the Host (e.g., smtp.gmail.com), Port (587), and choose Encryption (TLS/SSL). Provide your SMTP User and Password.
  • SendGrid API: Simply paste your SendGrid API Key to connect instantly.

[IMAGE: Email Configuration screen showing Provider Engine and Sender Identity settings]

Step 1: Campaign Details

In the first section, you define the core identity of your campaign. This includes the internal name for organization and the public subject line that users will see in their inbox.

  • Campaign Name: A descriptive name to identify the campaign within your dashboard.
  • Email Subject: The subject line of the email. Keep it catchy to improve open rates.

[IMAGE: Campaign Details form with Name and Subject fields]

Step 2: Selecting Audience

Choose exactly who will receive your email. You can target specific contact lists or behave-based segments created in the Contact Management module.

  • Contact Lists: Select one or more pre-defined folders of contacts.
  • Segments: Filter your audience based on specific criteria or tags for higher relevance.

[IMAGE: Audience selection panel showing Lists and Segments checkboxes]

Step 3: Email Content & AI Generation

Design your email using the built-in HTML editor or leverage the power of **AI Generation** to draft high-quality content based on a simple prompt.

  • Generate with AI: Enter a prompt (e.g., "Draft a promotional email for our summer sale") and let AI write the content for you.
  • HTML Content: Manually edit or paste your HTML code, with a real-time preview to ensure everything looks perfect.

[IMAGE: Content editor with AI Generation prompt field and HTML preview mode]

Step 4: Review & Launch

The final step allows you to verify your campaign settings and decide when the emails should be sent.

  • Immediate Sending: Launch the campaign instantly to reach your audience right away.
  • Scheduled Sending: Choose a specific date and time for the campaign to launch automatically. This is ideal for planning across different timezones.

[IMAGE: Final Review screen with Summary details and Scheduling options]
