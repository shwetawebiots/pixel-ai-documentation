Social Media Dashboard

Operational Overview

The Social Media Suite Dashboard provides a centralized command center for your digital presence. It combines real-time interaction analytics with automated scheduling monitoring, ensuring your social strategy is data-driven and efficient.

1. Real-time Interaction Metrics

The top row of the dashboard displays critical performance indicators across all connected platforms:

  • Total Reach: Cumulative visibility of your content across Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
  • Scheduled: Total count of posts currently queued for future deployment.
  • Published: Total number of successful deployments within the current operational cycle.
  • Platform Followers: Individual breakdowns for active networks showing specific audience growth.

[IMAGE: Top metric cards showing Reach, Scheduled, and Published stats]

2. Recent Operations Stream

The Recent Posts card provides a live feed of your latest activity. Each entry includes a visual preview, the target platform (indicated by the network icon), and a real-time status marker.

  • Published (Green): Content has been successfully deployed to the destination network.
  • Failed (Orange): Deployment was interrupted. You can view the operational archive for detailed error logs.

[IMAGE: Recent Posts grid showing image previews and status badges]

3. Advanced Analytical Engines

Pixel AI uses advanced data visualization to help you understand engagement patterns:

Engagement Candlestick (OHLC Analysis)

A sophisticated visualization showing open, high, low, and close engagement metrics across distributed nodes, allowing you to identify bullish or bearish engagement trends.

Temporal Grid & Pulse Broadcast
  • Temporal Grid: A high-level calendar overview of all scheduled and published deployments.
  • Pulse Broadcast: Trigger immediate multi-node network broadcasts for breaking updates.
  • Optimal Cluster: AI-detected peak interaction windows, providing an efficiency score to help you time your posts perfectly.

[IMAGE: Engagement Candlestick chart and Optimal Cluster metrics]
